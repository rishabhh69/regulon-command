## 🎬 Y Combinator Application Components

**Y Combinator asks for**:

1. **What problem are you solving?** (500 words)
   - Example: "Indian CAs spend 8 hours on GST filing with 20% error rate"

2. **Why are you uniquely suited?** (500 words)
   - Example: "Founder was CA for 10 years, understands pain deeply"

3. **What have you achieved so far?** (500 words)
   - Example: "20 paying customers, ₹50K MRR, 10% weekly growth"

4. **What will you do in the next 3 months?** (500 words)
   - Example: "100 customers, ₹300K MRR, hire 2 engineers"

5. **Pitch deck** (15-20 slides)

6. **Demo video** (1 minute showing product)

7. **Founder bios** (150-200 words each)

**Application deadline**: Depends on batch (check YC website)  
**Acceptance rate**: ~5% (1 in 20 applications)  
**Why YC matters**: ₹2Cr seed funding, credibility, network, mentor access

## ⚖️ Y Combinator Deal Structure

**If accepted, YC will offer**:
- **Funding**: ₹2 Cr (approximately, varies by batch)
- **Equity taken**: 7% of your company
- **In exchange for**: 3-month intensive program (SF or remote)
- **Network**: 1,000+ YC founders, investors, partners
- **Mentorship**: Weekly office hours with experienced founders

**Worth it?** Yes, 100%. Even the 7% is worth the brand credibility + network.

## 🎓 Key Learnings from Successful YC Companies

**What Y Combinator looks for**:
1. **Founder quality** (50%) - Vision, grit, execution
2. **Product quality** (30%) - Does it solve real problem?
3. **Market opportunity** (20%) - How big can it get?

**How to stand out**:
- Be specific with numbers (not "millions of CAs" but "2.4M CAs in India")
- Show real traction (paying customers > waitlist)
- Tell your personal story (not just product features)
- Address the biggest risk head-on (don't hide it)
- Have an unfair advantage (regulatory knowledge, founder credibility)
